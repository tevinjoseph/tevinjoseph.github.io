Can India retain world cup?

Fri 02 January 2015

Of course, India have the ability to retain the world cup. The key players will be Kohli, Raina, Bhuvneshwar Kumar and captain Dhoni himself. If Kohli is in full flow, no opposition can stop him. What is the specialty of this young champion player is, if he gets off to a good start, he makes sure he will be there at the end of the innings. The ability to score centuries at ease make him one of the best in the world.

Then comes Suresh Raina, he is a complete package for every captain. A brilliant fielder, good batsman and partnership breaker too. If he gets a chance to bat NO.4 for above, he will win a lot of matches for you.

We all know the finishing talent of Dhoni. He finishes things in style. His captaincy and keeping will be crucial for India. On his day, no one can compete with Rohit Sharma. He has got some serious talent. If he can iron out his laziness, as Kohli said earlier he will be the X-factor for India in the world cup.

Dhawan has some problems against short pitch deliveries, but he too can play a major role. Rahane/Rayudu can provide stability in the middle order. The likes of Aswin, Jadeja/Akshar Patel can bat too.

Only one of Aswin, Jadeja and Akshar Patel will play in the world cup because the pitches in Australia and New Zealand support more bounce. Patel has an edge over others considering his recent success.

Bhuvi and Umesh Yadav will lead the pace bowling department. Bhuvi can swing the bowl in both ways while Umesh bowls in express pace. Varun Aron also can add variety to the Indian attack. Let's not forget Shami who is the highest wicket taker for India in recent times.

Now, with Raina, Kohli, Jadeja and Rahane India is a world class fielding unit and this makes India a balanced side. The bouncy pitches are a bit of concern but, believe me, these guys won't disappoint you.
